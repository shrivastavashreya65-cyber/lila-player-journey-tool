**Gameplay Insights**

**Insight 1: Combat Clusters Around High-Mobility Map Corridors**

_**Observation**_

The kill heatmaps consistently show combat clustering along major road intersections and bridge crossings, particularly in Ambrose Valley. These locations act as natural choke points where multiple player paths converge.


_**Evidence**_

Using the player journey visualization and kill heatmaps, several matches reveal overlapping player paths converging near the central bridge area. This results in a significantly higher density of kill markers compared to surrounding regions.


_**Why This Happens**_

Players naturally gravitate toward:

- high mobility routes (roads)

- map traversal shortcuts (bridges and crossings)

- areas that connect multiple regions

These routes unintentionally become forced engagement zones, increasing early combat probability.


_**Product Implication**_

While some choke points are desirable, overly dominant combat clusters can reduce gameplay variety by forcing similar engagements across matches.


_**Actionable Recommendation**_

Level designers could experiment with:

- Adding alternative traversal routes around choke points

- Redistributing loot spawn locations slightly away from these intersections

- Introducing cover or terrain variation to diversify combat dynamics
- 

_**Metrics Impacted**_

- Player survival time

- Combat distribution across map regions

- Early match elimination rates
  

**Insight 2: Peripheral Areas Show Consistently Low Player Traffic**

_**Observation**_

Movement heatmaps reveal that outer map regions receive significantly lower player traffic, with most player paths concentrated toward central areas and known loot locations.

_**Evidence**_

Player path overlays show repeated traversal patterns through similar map corridors, while certain outer zones remain largely unused across multiple matches.

_**Why This Happens**_

Players tend to prioritize:

- central points of interest

- high-loot zones

- efficient extraction paths

If peripheral areas lack meaningful incentives, players naturally avoid them.


_**Product Implication**_

Underused map areas represent missed gameplay potential and can make maps feel smaller than intended.


_**Actionable Recommendation**_

Design interventions could include:

- Introducing high-value loot spawns in underused regions

- Adding secondary objectives or extraction points

- Adjusting environmental storytelling or landmarks to encourage exploration


_**Metrics Impacted**_

- Map exploration rate

- Average player path diversity

- Engagement distribution across the map



**Insight 3: Storm Deaths Suggest Players Delay Rotation Too Long**

_**Observation**_

Storm deaths appear frequently near map edges, indicating that players often remain in outer regions until the storm becomes dangerous.


_**Evidence**_

Storm death markers cluster along outer map zones where players appear to delay movement toward safer areas.


_**Why This Happens**_

Possible reasons include:

- Players prioritizing loot collection over safe rotation

- Storm progression speed being underestimated

- Limited safe traversal routes from outer areas

_**Product Implication**_

If storm deaths occur too frequently, players may feel punished rather than challenged.

_**Actionable Recommendation**_

- Potential improvements could include:

- Stronger visual or audio warnings for storm progression

- Slight timing adjustments to storm speed

- Additional rotation paths from outer areas

_**Metrics Impacted**_

- Storm death rate

- Average match survival duration

- Player frustration indicators (rage quits, early exits)
